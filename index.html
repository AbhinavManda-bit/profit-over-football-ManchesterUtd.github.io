Historical Context

From the tragedy of Munich to decades of dominance under Busby and Ferguson, Manchester United’s identity was built on resilience and sustained success.

1958: Munich Air Disaster

A tragedy that shaped the club’s spirit and future.

1968: European Champions

Just ten years after Munich, United won the European Cup, a defining symbol of resilience and rebirth.

1960s–2000s: Sustained Success

13 Premier League titles, 2 Champions League titles, 5 FA Cups, and global dominance.

2005: The Glazer Takeover

A leveraged buyout loads the club with more than £500 million in debt.

2010: Green & Gold Protests

Fans unite against the ownership model.

2013: Post-Takeover Decline

No Premier League titles since his retirement.

2024: Record Low Premier League Finish

Before 2005, United’s lowest Premier League finish was 3rd place with 75 points in 2003–04. By 2024–25, the club had fallen to 15th with 42 points, the worst placement and points total in their Premier League history.

The Shift

Before 2005, Manchester United was a debt-free club, reinvesting profits into the squad and stadium.

The Glazer family’s takeover changed everything.

By using loans secured against the club’s assets to buy it, they transferred their debt onto the club. Since then, over £1 billion has been drained in interest, dividends, and fees.

The Cost of Debt

While revenues have soared, debt servicing has crippled the club’s ability to compete in the transfer market and maintain infrastructure.

The Soul of the Club
Is Being Sold

Manchester United was born from the railway workers of Newton Heath. Today, the connection between the club and its local community is fraying. Protests are not just about results; they are a fight for the club’s soul.

The Path Forward

Change is possible. Here are the structural reforms needed to restore Manchester United to its former glory.

Partial Fan Ownership

Implementing a 50+1 model or golden share to give fans voting rights on key decisions.

Independent Regulator

Stronger financial oversight to prevent leveraged buyouts and ensure sustainable ownership.

Investment Commitments

Legally binding pledges to invest in stadium infrastructure and youth development.
